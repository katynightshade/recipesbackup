Treacle Tart

Photo from The Unlikely Baker

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup confectioner's sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 sticks cold butter cut into chunks
  • 2 cold large egg yolks
  • ⅓ cup cold heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 egg beaten with 1 tablespoon water, for brushing over the crust

Filling:

  • 1 cup golden syrup or light molasses or corn syrup
  • 2 ¼ cups fresh bread crumbs
  • Grated zest and juice of 1 lemon

Steps:

  1. For the crust, place the flour, confectioner's sugar, and salt in the bowl of a food processor and pulse to combine. Scatter the pieces of butter over the flour mixture.
  2. Pulse until the mixture resembles coarse yellow meal without any white powdery bits remaining, about 20 pulses.
  3. Transfer the mixture to a large mixing bowl. Beat the egg yolks with the cream and vanilla and pour them into the flour-butter mixture. Toss with a spatula until the dough clumps together. If the dough is dry, add 1 more tablespoon heavy cream (better too wet than too dry).
  4. Divide the dough in half, form into disks, wrap in plastic wrap, and chill at least 2 hours or up to 3 days.
  5. Just before you are ready to roll out the dough, prepare the filling and preheat the oven to 400°F. Warm the golden syrup in a saucepan until it is runny, or microwave it for 1 minute. Combine the golden syrup, bread crumbs, lemon zest, and lemon juice in a mixing bowl and mix well.
  6. Remove the dough from the refrigerator. On a floured surface, roll out the larger disk to an 11-inch circle.
  7. Fold it into quarters, brush off the excess flour with a pastry brush after each fold, then unfold it into a 9-inch tart pan, easing the sides gently into the pan and pressing the dough into the fluted edges. Trim the dough even with the rim.
  8. Roll out the second disk 1/8-inch thick. Cut the dough in long strips for the lattice topping.
  9. Scrape the filling into the prepared crust and smooth the top with a rubber spatula.
  10. Lay half the strips of dough over the filling in one direction and the other half in the opposite direction to form a lattice. Trim the overhang. Gently brush the beaten egg over the lattice.
  11. Bake for 10 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 375F and bake another 25 minutes until the crust is browned and the filling puffs up in the centre.
